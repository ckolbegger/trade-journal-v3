# Strategy as template; deviations measured against the original Plan

A Strategy is a declared template, not a classification: it pre-fills the Plan's structured Planned Legs and declares which Exit Levels its plan form asks for. An Exit Level has a scope (whole Trade, or one Leg — "buy back the short call at 20% of its credit") and a kind (underlying price; structure value; % of max profit; trailing = high-water minus offset). The trailing high-water is the running maximum of the scoped structure's signed value — each Leg contributes quantity × price with shorts negative, summed within the same day's Marks (per-leg extremes from different days are never combined; that would trail a value the Trade never had). For plain stock this reduces to the underlying's high-water. Always derived from Mark history, never stored; with manual Marks it tracks daily closes (understating intraday trailing stops) and upgrades when Daily Bars arrive. P&L and structural risk/reward computation never read Strategy — one universal formula over Legs, Executions, and Marks handles debit and credit structures identically, because buy/sell direction carries the signs.

Deviations (structural: unplanned leg; sizing: quantity beyond plan; discipline: stop/target crossed without action) are auto-detected against the original Plan and recorded at the moment of detection — structural/sizing when the triggering Execution is saved, discipline when Daily Review first surfaces them. Detection is a pure computation (TradeMath); recording belongs to the Books. Recorded Deviations are acknowledgeable, journal-linkable, and immutable — tuning detection math later never rewrites adherence history. They remain recorded even when a Plan Revision documents the change of intent — adherence analytics need the fact of departure, and Revision timestamps distinguish planned adaptation (revised, then executed) from rationalization (executed, then revised). Time-horizon overrun is deliberately not a Deviation; it surfaces via the "Timed Out" Close Reason instead.

Performance analytics group by declaration (Strategy, Tags) and by derived dimensions computed from the data (e.g., net opening cash flow classifies every spread as credit or debit), so cross-cutting questions never depend on labeling discipline.
