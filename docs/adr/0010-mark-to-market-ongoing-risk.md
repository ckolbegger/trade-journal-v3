# Ongoing risk/reward is mark-to-market, not plan-anchored

Ongoing Risk measures from today's Marks to the Trade's worst case — unrealized gains count as the trader's money and holding re-risks them — and Incremental Reward measures from today's Marks to the best case. In a bull put spread sold for $2.00 now marked $0.50, the app says "risking $950 to make $50," not "risking $800 with $50 left." The plan-anchored framing (broker convention) was rejected because it hides the giveback and blunts the exit signal; the original Plan's risk/reward is displayed alongside for contrast.

Both sides carry two anchors, symmetrically: risk is shown to the Plan's stop (Planned Risk) and to the structural extreme (Worst-Case Risk — stock to zero, spread beyond the long strike; may be unlimited); reward is shown to the Plan's target (Planned Reward) and to the structural extreme (Max Reward; may be unlimited). A stop is an intention, not a floor — neither anchor substitutes for the other.
